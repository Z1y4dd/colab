XRF Lab Element Descriptions
============================

Each of the following variables represents the concentration of a chemical element measured using X-Ray Fluorescence (XRF).
These are used in geochemical analysis of core samples to infer mineralogical, depositional, and diagenetic information.

----------------------------
Major Elements (Rock Formers)
----------------------------

 Sodium (Na)
  - Common in feldspars (albite) and evaporites.
  - Indicates salinity or hydrothermal alteration.

 Magnesium (Mg)
  - Present in dolomite, chlorite, and serpentine.
  - Associated with carbonates or mafic inputs.

Lab_XRF_Al  - Aluminum (Al)
  - Found in clays (kaolinite, illite) and feldspars.
  - High values may indicate shaliness or alteration.

 Silicon (Si)
  - Primary component of quartz and silicates.
  - High Si usually means quartz-rich, clean sandstones.

 Phosphorus (P)
  - Typically from apatite or organics.
  - Can point to phosphatic or organic-rich zones.

 Sulfur (S)
  - Found in pyrite, gypsum, and other sulfides/sulfates.
  - Indicates reducing environments, sour gas potential.

 Chlorine (Cl)
  - Present in halite or brine-saturated samples.
  - Associated with saline fluids or evaporites.

 Potassium (K)
  - Common in illite, K-feldspar, and mica.
  - Useful for clay typing and correlates with gamma ray logs.

 Calcium (Ca)
  - Dominant in calcite, aragonite, dolomite.
  - Indicates carbonate content or diagenesis.

-------------------------------
Minor / Trace Elements (Diagnostic)
-------------------------------

 Titanium (Ti)
  - Occurs in heavy minerals (e.g., rutile).
  - Indicates detrital input or sediment maturity.

 Manganese (Mn)
  - Found in carbonates and oxides.
  - Redox-sensitive; may reflect diagenesis or water chemistry shifts.

 Iron (Fe)
  - Present in pyrite, hematite, siderite, clays.
  - Indicates redox conditions and mineral alteration.

 Zirconium (Zr)
  - Associated with zircon.
  - Reflects sediment provenance and maturity.

 Strontium (Sr)
  - Substitutes for Ca in carbonates.
  - Sr/Ca ratio is useful for paleoenvironment analysis.

 Barium (Ba)
  - Found in barite.
  - Indicates hydrothermal fluids or organic-rich deposition zones.
